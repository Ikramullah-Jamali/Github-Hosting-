CURRICULUM VITA

A Curriculum Vitae (CV) is a formal document that provides a detailed summary of a person’s education, skills, experiences, and accomplishments. The term "Curriculum Vitae" is Latin and means “course of life.” A CV is commonly used when applying for jobs, internships, scholarships, or academic programs. Unlike a résumé, which is short and targeted, a CV is more comprehensive and detailed, especially for academic or research positions. It includes personal details, educational background, A well-written CV helps the employer understand who you are, what you have done, and how suitable you are for the position. It should be clear, organized, and truthful, with correct contact information so the employer can reach you. There is no strict length limit for a CV, but it should be relevant and focused. Fresh graduates may have a 1–2 page CV, while experienced professionals or researchers may have longer CVs. A CV usually contains the following sections:Personal InformationCareer ObjectiveEducational Background Work or Internship ExperienceSkills and LanguagesAchievements or AwardsHobbies/InterestsReferences (if required) In today’s digital world, CVs can be created using software tools or even coded as web pages using HTML and CSS. An impressive CV creates a strong first impression and increases the chances of being selected for interviews.
